What Are Sweepstakes Casinos

If you've been living under a rock for the last few years, here's the deal: sweepstakes casinos are online gaming sites that operate under US sweepstakes law instead of gambling law. You don't bet real money directly. Instead, you play with two currencies — Gold Coins (GC) for fun and Sweeps Coins (SC) that can be redeemed for actual cash.

The legal framework is pretty straightforward. Traditional online gambling is illegal in most states. But sweepstakes promotions? Those are federally legal under the same laws that let McDonald's run Monopoly games. As long as there's a free entry method (no purchase necessary), it's a sweepstakes — not gambling.

Here's how it works in practice:

  • You sign up for free. Every legit sweepstakes casino gives you some SC just for creating an account. No credit card needed.
  • You collect daily bonuses. Log in each day and claim free SC and GC. This is the core of the daily login hustle — and why sites like ours exist.
  • You play slots, table games, originals. Use your SC on real casino games from real providers. Same slots you'd find at a DraftKings or FanDuel casino.
  • You redeem SC for cash. Hit the minimum threshold (anywhere from $5 to $100 depending on the site), submit a redemption request, and get real money sent to your bank or PayPal.

The catch? There isn't really one. You can legitimately play and cash out without spending anything. The casinos make their money from people who buy Gold Coin packages (which come with "bonus" SC as a promotional add-on). But if you're disciplined about just collecting free SC, you're basically getting paid to click a button every day.
