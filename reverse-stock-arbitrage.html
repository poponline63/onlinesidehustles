📊 What is RSA (Reverse Stock Arbitrage)?

Stocks listed on major exchanges like the NASDAQ are required to maintain a minimum share price of $1.00. When a stock falls below that threshold, the company can perform a reverse stock split to bring the price back up. This creates a unique profit opportunity for savvy investors.

🔄
What's a Reverse Split?
A reverse split consolidates multiple shares into one higher-value share. Example: A 1:10 reverse split converts 10 shares at $0.10 each into 1 share worth $1.00.
💰
How You Profit
Your brokerage rounds your fractional share to 1 full share.

Example: Your single $0.67 share will round up to the new single share amount of $6.70 meaning you profit $6.03 per account, this is in the case of a 1:10 split which is most typical.
🔑
The Key to Success
You can do this only once per account and per stock, so you can exponentially increase your profits by opening up multiple accounts on multiple brokerages.

So if you have 20 accounts suddenly your profit turns into $6.70 x 20 Accounts = $134.

🚀 How to Maximize Your Profits

Multiple accounts = Multiply your gains!

By opening multiple brokerage accounts, you can purchase 1 share per account, allowing you to enter each RSA opportunity multiple times. Each account earns the rounding profit independently, so 25 accounts means 25x the profit on every opportunity.

⚠️ Is There Any Risk?

✅ Profit Risk
Minimal—you'll typically profit on every trade or break even at worst. In the past, some brokerages used "clawbacks" to reclaim RSA profits, causing minor losses. However, this practice has been prohibited for brokerages in the last six months.
⚠️ Risk of Account Bans
There is a small risk if you abuse the system with excessive accounts. Users with 100+ accounts have been banned from Apex-backed brokerages. If you have important accounts (like a 401K with Fidelity), avoid creating 100+ accounts there.

Most people doing this as a side hustle won't face issues and can profit reliably. As with any side hustle—whether sweepstakes, stocks, or crypto—there's always some risk. Assess what you're comfortable with. Personally, I've used this method and have been happy with the results.
