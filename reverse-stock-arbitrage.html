What is RSA (Reverse Split Arbitrage)?

RSA is a powerful profit strategy—the more accounts you use, the more you can earn per opportunity.

📊

Why This Works

Stocks must maintain a minimum price of $1.00 per share to stay listed on major exchanges. When a company's stock price drops too low, they often perform a reverse split to boost the share price and maintain compliance.

🔄

What's a Reverse Split?

A reverse split consolidates multiple shares into one higher-value share.

Example: A 1:10 reverse split converts 10 shares at $0.10 each into 1 share worth $1.00.

💰

How You Profit

When you own just 1 share before a reverse split:

  • Best Case: The brokerage rounds your fractional share UP to 1 full new share, giving you instant profit
  • Example: Your $0.67 share becomes worth $6.70 after a 1:10 split!
  • Worst Case: You receive a fractional share and break even

🔑 The Key to Success

One share per account = Guaranteed rounding profits

You're limited to 1 transaction per RSA opportunity per account, which is why multiple accounts = multiply your gains!

How to Maximize Your Profits

Multiple accounts = Multiply your gains!

📈 Scaling Your Income

By opening multiple brokerage accounts, you can purchase 1 share per account, allowing you to enter each RSA opportunity multiple times.

The more accounts you have, the more profit you can make per reverse split event. It's a simple numbers game that scales with your effort.

Is There Any Risk?

⚠️ Understanding the Risks

Profit Risk: Minimal
You'll typically profit on every trade or break even at worst. In the past, some brokerages used "clawbacks" to reclaim RSA profits, causing minor losses. However, this practice has been prohibited for brokerages in the last six months.

Risk of Account Bans:
There is a small risk if you abuse the system with excessive accounts:

  • Users with 100+ accounts have been banned from Apex-backed brokerages

Most people doing this as a side hustle won't face issues and can profit reliably. As with any side hustle—whether sweepstakes, stocks, or crypto—there's always some risk. Assess what you're comfortable with.
