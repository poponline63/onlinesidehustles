📊 What is RSA (Reverse Stock Arbitrage)?

Stocks listed on major exchanges like the NASDAQ are required to maintain a minimum share price of $1.00. When a stock falls below that threshold, the company can perform a reverse stock split to bring the price back up. This creates a unique profit opportunity for savvy investors.

🔄
What's a Reverse Split?
A reverse split consolidates multiple shares into one higher-value share. Example: A 1:10 reverse split converts 10 shares at $0.10 each into 1 share worth $1.00.
💰
How You Profit
Your brokerage rounds your fractional share to 1 full share.

Example: Your single $0.67 share will round up to the new single share amount of $6.70 meaning you profit $6.03 per account, this is in the case of a 1:10 split which is most typical.
🔑
The Key to Success
You can do this only once per account and per stock, so you can exponentially increase your profits by opening up multiple accounts on multiple brokerages.

So if you have 20 accounts suddenly your profit turns into $6.70 x 20 Accounts = $134.

⚠️ Is There Any Risk?

✅ Profit Risk
Minimal—you'll typically profit on every trade. In the worst case, you receive a fractional share where you will break even or at most lose a couple of cents per share.
