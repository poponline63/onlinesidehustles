💰 Payouts & Money

How do I redeem Sweeps Coins for real money? ▼
Each site has a minimum redemption amount (usually $50–$100 in SC). Once you hit it and have completed any playthrough requirements, go to the site's cashier or redemption page and request a withdrawal. Payment methods include:
  • Bank transfer — Most common, 1–5 business days
  • PayPal — Available on some sites
  • Skrill — 0% fees on eligible sites
  • Gift cards — Lower minimums ($10–$25)
  • Cryptocurrency — Sites like Stake.us, Shuffle.us
What is a playthrough / wagering requirement? ▼
A playthrough requirement means you need to wager your SC a certain number of times before it becomes redeemable. For example, a 1x playthrough on 10 SC means you need to place $10 worth of bets before you can cash out.

Most daily login bonuses have no playthrough or a simple 1x requirement. Welcome packages usually have 1x–3x. The key is to play high-RTP games (96–98% return) during playthrough so you keep most of your SC. Check our glossary for more details.
What is KYC and will I need to verify my identity? ▼
KYC (Know Your Customer) is identity verification required before you can cash out. It's a legal requirement. You'll typically need:
  • Government-issued photo ID (driver's license or passport)
  • Proof of address (utility bill or bank statement)
  • Sometimes a selfie for facial verification
Pro tip: Complete KYC as soon as you sign up so there's no delay when you're ready to redeem. Some sites verify you instantly; others take 24–72 hours.
Do I have to pay taxes on sweepstakes winnings? ▼
In the US, sweepstakes winnings are considered taxable income. Sites may issue a 1099 form for winnings above $600 in a calendar year. Keep records of your purchases (which can offset winnings for tax purposes) and consult a tax professional for your specific situation. This is standard for any prize winnings — it's no different from winning a raffle.

🔒 Legality & Safety

Are sweepstakes casinos legal in my state? ▼
Sweepstakes casinos are legal in most US states. They operate under sweepstakes/promotional laws, not gambling regulations. The most commonly restricted states are:
  • Washington — Restricted on most sites
  • Idaho — Restricted on most sites
  • Michigan, Nevada, Connecticut, Montana — Restricted on some sites
Each site has its own restricted states list. Check the terms of service during signup — the site will tell you if you're eligible. If you're in a restricted state for one site, there are plenty of others that accept your state.
Is this the same as online gambling? ▼
No. Sweepstakes casinos are legally distinct from online gambling. The key difference is the "no purchase necessary" element — you can always obtain Sweeps Coins for free (through daily logins, mail-in offers, social media promotions). The purchase of Gold Coins is for entertainment, and SC comes as a bonus. This dual-currency model is what makes them legal under sweepstakes law.

Can I use multiple accounts on sweepstakes sites? ▼
No — this will get you banned. Every site has strict one-account-per-person policies. They verify through KYC, IP addresses, and device fingerprinting. If caught with multiple accounts, you'll lose all your coins and get permanently banned. However, different people in the same household can usually each have their own account (like a spouse or adult family member), as long as each passes independent KYC.
