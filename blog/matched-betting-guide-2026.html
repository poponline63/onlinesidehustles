What Is Matched Betting?

Matched betting is a strategy that uses sportsbook free bet promotions to generate guaranteed profit. Instead of picking winners — which is gambling — you place two opposing bets that cover both outcomes of an event. The result cancels out, and what's left is the profit from the free bonus itself.

It originated in the UK where it's been mainstream for 10+ years, with thousands of people earning their entire income from it. In the US it's newer but the same logic applies — sportsbooks are throwing hundreds of dollars at every new customer to win market share, and matched bettors collect that money systematically.

💡 Simple Example — DraftKings

DraftKings offers: "Bet $5, get $150 in bonus bets if your first bet wins."
You bet $5 on Team A to win at odds of +100 (even money).
You simultaneously bet $5 against Team A on a second book.
If Team A wins: you get $150 bonus bets, cover your lay loss (~$5). Net: ~+$112 in free bets extracted.
If Team A loses: your lay bet covers the $5 loss. Net: ~$0 out of pocket, offer not triggered — you try again.
Either way, your downside is essentially zero. The upside is $112 in real profit.

⚠️ This Is NOT Gambling

Gamblers win or lose based on the outcome. Matched bettors cover both outcomes — they don't care who wins. The profit is extracted from the bonus system, not from predicting sports. Treated as a business process, this is as close to guaranteed income as side hustles get.

How It Works — The Two-Bet System

Every matched bet has two parts:

  • The Back Bet: Bet FOR an outcome (e.g., Team A wins) at the sportsbook offering the bonus
  • The Lay Bet: Bet AGAINST the same outcome (e.g., Team A doesn't win) at a betting exchange or second book

These two bets cancel each other out financially. You've now unlocked the promotional bonus without taking on any real sporting risk.

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Find a sign-up offer — DraftKings, FanDuel, BetMGM, Caesars, BetRivers, etc. All have new-user promotions.
  2. Calculate the required qualifying bet — use an odds matching calculator to find the best market to minimize your qualifying loss.
  3. Place your back bet at the sportsbook — on a near 50/50 market (a spread at -110/-110 is ideal).
  4. Place your lay bet at Sporttrade, Prophet Exchange, or a second sportsbook at close to the same odds.
  5. Let the bet settle. You receive your bonus bet credit.
  6. Convert the bonus bet to cash — place the free bet on a higher-odds market (like +300 or higher), cover the lay side. You extract ~70–80% of the free bet as real cash regardless of outcome.
  7. Repeat with the next sportsbook.

US-Specific Considerations

Betting Exchanges

The UK uses Betfair for lay betting. In the US, your main options are:

  • Sporttrade — available in NJ, PA, CO, AZ, and expanding. True exchange with lay betting.
  • Prophet Exchange — newer, growing state list. No-vig exchange model.
  • Second sportsbook as hedge — if no exchange is available in your state, use a second sportsbook running the opposite promo to hedge instead.

State Availability

Legal sports betting is live in 35+ states. Check your state — if it's legal, you have access to multiple sportsbook sign-up offers. Each one is money on the table.

Taxes

US sportsbook winnings are taxable. Keep a log of all qualifying bets, lay bets, and bonus bet conversions. Report net gambling winnings on Schedule 1. Talk to a tax professional if you're extracting significant amounts.

Account Restrictions ("Gubbing")

Sportsbooks can limit or ban accounts they identify as bonus abusers. To stay under the radar:

  • Don't always take maximum promo amounts
  • Place occasional small recreational bets alongside your matched bets
  • Bet on popular, mainstream markets — not obscure events
  • Don't withdraw immediately after every bonus — let money sit briefly

Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not using a float — you need capital moving between accounts. Start with $200–$500 so you always have funds available. This money isn't spent, just circulated.
  • Rushing qualifying bets — pick markets near 50/50 odds (-110/-110) to minimize your qualifying loss. Don't bet +500 underdogs as your qualifying bet.
  • Skipping the lay bet — this turns matched betting into actual gambling. Always cover the other side before the event starts.
  • Betting on the same event you've laid — if you forget to place your lay, don't panic-place it during the game when odds have moved. Eat the qualifying loss and wait for the next opportunity.
  • Withdrawing instantly every time — flags your account. Let the float sit naturally and withdraw periodically.
  • Not tracking everything — use a spreadsheet. Record every back bet, lay bet, odds, stake, and outcome. You need this for taxes and to know exactly where your float is at all times.
  • Doing this in an illegal betting state — check your state's legal status first. If sports betting isn't legal where you live, this strategy isn't available to you yet.
