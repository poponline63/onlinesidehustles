📊 Understanding RTP (Return to Player)

RTP is the percentage of money a game returns to players over time. A 96% RTP slot means that for every $100 wagered, the game returns $96 on average (the house keeps $4).

Key things to understand about RTP:

  • It's a long-term average — In any single session, you could win big or lose everything. RTP plays out over thousands of spins.
  • Higher RTP = less loss over time — This is especially important when you need to play through SC to meet redemption requirements.
  • Table games generally have higher RTP than slots — Blackjack with basic strategy is 99.5%, while most slots are 92-96%.
  • Some games don't count toward playthrough — Many sites exclude table games or weight them at 10-20% of slots. Always check the terms.

How to Find a Game's RTP

Most sweepstakes casinos display RTP in the game info/rules section. Click the "i" or "?" icon within the game. If it's not shown, Google "[game name] RTP" — providers like Pragmatic Play publish RTPs on their official sites. Check our glossary for more terminology.

💰 Bankroll Management

Whether you're playing with free SC or bought packages, managing your bankroll properly is what separates winners from people who burn through their balance.

Rule #1: The 1-2% Rule

Never bet more than 1-2% of your total SC balance on a single game or spin. If you have 500 SC, your max bet should be 5-10 SC. This prevents variance from wiping you out and gives you enough spins for the RTP to work in your favor.

Rule #2: Set Win/Loss Limits

Before each session, decide:

  • Win limit: "I'll stop if I'm up 30% from my starting balance"
  • Loss limit: "I'll stop if I've lost 20% of my session bankroll"

Hitting either limit means you stop. No exceptions. The biggest losses come from chasing after a bad streak or getting greedy during a good one.

Rule #3: Separate Your Bankrolls

Keep your "daily bonus SC" separate from your "playthrough SC" mentally (or track in a spreadsheet). Daily bonuses are pure profit — don't gamble them away on slots. If you want to play for fun, use the Gold Coins.

⚠️ Common Traps to Avoid

❌ Trap #1: Chasing Losses

You lost 50 SC on a bad slot session. The impulse is to bet bigger to "win it back." Don't. This is exactly how real money casinos profit from people. Stick to your bankroll rules and walk away. The daily bonuses will replenish your balance over time.

❌ Trap #2: Playing High-Volatility Slots with Playthrough SC

High-volatility slots can pay massive jackpots, but they also drain your balance fast. When you need to play through SC to meet redemption requirements, ALWAYS use low-volatility, high-RTP games. Save the flashy slots for Gold Coins.

❌ Trap #3: Ignoring Playthrough Weightings

Some sites weight game contributions differently. For example, slots might count 100% toward playthrough while blackjack only counts 10%. This means you'd need to wager 10x more at blackjack to clear the same playthrough. Always check the terms before you start playing through.

Not every package is a good deal. Some give you terrible SC-to-dollar ratios. Always do the math before purchasing. If the effective return after playthrough is less than 100%, it's not worth it. Our churning guide shows you exactly how to evaluate packages.
