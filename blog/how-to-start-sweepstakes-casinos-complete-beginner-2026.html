🎲 What Are Sweepstakes Casinos?

Sweepstakes casinos are a legal way to play casino-style games — slots, blackjack, roulette, poker — and win real cash in 49 US states (Washington state is the only exception). They are not traditional online gambling. They operate under promotional sweepstakes laws, the same legal model that makes McDonald’s Monopoly or Publisher’s Clearing House possible.

The key difference is the dual-currency system:

  • Gold Coins (GC) — the entertainment currency. You use these to play games for fun. They have zero cash value and cannot be redeemed.
  • Sweeps Coins (SC) — the valuable currency. Each SC is worth approximately $1 and can be redeemed for real cash. Different sites call them different things (Crown Coins, Lucky Coins, Fortune Coins), but the concept is identical everywhere.

Every sweepstakes casino gives you free Sweeps Coins just for logging in daily. No purchase is ever required. You collect these daily bonuses, build up your balance, and cash out when you hit the site’s minimum redemption threshold.

💡 Why Is This Legal?

Sweepstakes casinos are classified as promotional sweepstakes, not gambling. The legal requirement is simple: you must always have a free way to get Sweeps Coins. Since every site offers free daily bonuses and mail-in entry options, they meet the sweepstakes threshold. That is why they are legal in 49 states without any gambling license. You do not need to buy anything. The entire strategy works on free daily bonuses. Do not purchase Gold Coin packages until you fully understand the system and have a strategy — like package churning.

Always sign up with your real name, real address, and real phone number. When you cash out, the site verifies your identity through KYC. If your account info does not match your government ID, you lose your entire balance permanently.
